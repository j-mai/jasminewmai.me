    About Me

    I'm currently a sophomore at Dartmouth College majoring in Computer Science modified with Digital Arts and minoring in Japanese and Human-Centered Design. I enjoy logically thinking through problems and being able come up with solutions that can have an impact on others, and for me, CS is the perfect medium to do so. I'm proficient in Python and Java, and I'm currently interested app development, virtual reality, and web development. As an animation enthusiast, I am also interested in using animation to tell stories.

    I'm intrigued by how media and technology can be used to create empathy. I have seen how voices can be trampled upon and silenced. I've realized that our biases impair our willingness to listen to the stories and struggles of others. In our increasingly polarized society, it is hard to look past our prejudices to start important conversations with people who have different perspectives. I believe that animation provides a place removed enough from reality where we can start these discussions and begin breaking the walls between people. I also think that virtual and augmented reality have strong potentials to help create a more connected and empathetic world because they can tell stories in an immersive, close environment.
